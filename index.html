Prototype Tool for Music Rights Management Launch

Revolutionary web-based tool for independent artists and small labels

üìÖ June 10, 2025 ‚è±Ô∏è 6 min read üë§ MC Media Development Team

We're excited to announce the launch of our prototype music rights management tool, designed specifically for independent artists and small record labels who need efficient copyright protection without the complexity of enterprise solutions.

Key Features of the Prototype

Our web-based platform allows artists to embed copyright information directly into their digital music files with just a few clicks. The tool automatically generates metadata, creates blockchain records for verification, and provides simple licensing templates for different use cases.

Beta Testing Results

During our closed beta with 50 independent artists, we saw a 95% success rate in copyright embedding and a 40% reduction in time spent on rights management tasks. Artists particularly appreciated the intuitive interface and automated documentation features.

What's Next

Based on user feedback, we're planning to integrate DAW plugins starting with Audacity, add collaborative features for band members, and develop mobile apps for on-the-go rights management. The full platform launch is scheduled for Q4 2025.

Interested in trying the prototype? Contact us at mcmedia.dk@gmail.com to join our expanded beta program.

Mobygratis Explained, A Discussion with Natalie Jacobs

Exploring free music licensing and its impact on creative industries

üìÖ May 28, 2025 ‚è±Ô∏è 45 min watch üë§ Natalie Jacobs & MC Media Team

In this fascinating discussion, Natalie Jacobs joins us to explore Moby's groundbreaking Mobygratis initiative and its implications for the future of music licensing and artist compensation.

The Mobygratis Model

Moby's decision to offer his music for free to independent filmmakers, students, and non-profits created a new paradigm for music licensing. Natalie breaks down how this model works, its benefits, and the challenges it presents for traditional rights management.

Impact on Independent Creators

We discuss how free licensing models can coexist with traditional copyright protection, and why having robust tracking systems becomes even more important when dealing with free-to-use content. The conversation covers attribution requirements, usage tracking, and revenue opportunities beyond direct licensing.

Key Takeaways

The discussion highlights the importance of flexible rights management systems that can handle both traditional licensing and alternative models like Mobygratis. Artists need tools that support their chosen distribution strategy while maintaining proper attribution and usage tracking.

This talk was recorded as part of our Industry Insights series. Watch the full conversation on our YouTube channel or listen to the podcast version on all major platforms.

Music Rights Clinic Launch

Free educational workshops and consultation services for artists

üìÖ June 15, 2025 ‚è±Ô∏è 4 min read üë§ MC Media Education Team

We're proud to announce the launch of our Music Rights Clinic, a free educational initiative designed to help independent artists understand and protect their creative works through hands-on workshops and one-on-one consultations.

What We Offer

The clinic provides free monthly workshops covering copyright basics, licensing strategies, revenue optimization, and practical tools for rights management. Each session includes hands-on experience with our platform and personalized guidance from industry experts.

Workshop Schedule

Monthly workshops are held both in-person in San Francisco and online for global accessibility. Topics rotate between beginner fundamentals, advanced licensing strategies, and technology tutorials. All sessions are recorded and made available to participants.

One-on-One Consultations

In addition to group workshops, we offer free 30-minute consultation calls for artists facing specific rights management challenges. Our team provides personalized advice on copyright registration, licensing negotiations, and platform optimization.

Community Impact

Since our pilot program began, we've helped over 200 independent artists properly register their works, negotiate better licensing deals, and implement effective rights management workflows. Our goal is to reach 1,000 artists by the end of 2025.

Our Services

Web-Based Copyright Embedding
